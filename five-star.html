Throne of Glass by Sarah J. Maas

Pages: 432

Dates read: unknown

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

This book was amazing. I read it as the second book in the series, just after The Assassin’s Blade. It leaves you with just enough to keep going. The way she gradually builds the 8-book world the reader is about to embark on is wonderful, and she does an amazing job planting seeds that will be very important later.

Crown of Midnight by Sarah J. Maas

Pages: 418

Dates read: Mar 15th to 19th

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Nothing in this series will get a rank lower than 5. It is a work of art. That being said, probably my least favorite book, but it is still amazing. My only problem with it is that this book does a lot of world-building, and it can feel like a lot and very confusing. I’m impatient, so reading this was stressful.

660 pages

One Dark Window by Rachel Gillig

392 pages

Dates read: Aug 14th to 23rd

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Really, I barely read the book, then read the whole thing on the 23rd. I was convinced there would be nothing as good as TOG, but I was wrong. Throne of Glass is already the best female main character fantasy series according to the fantasy community, and of course, her other two series come next, but this is up there. The world-building OMG. I did not think I would be this invested in anything ever again!
